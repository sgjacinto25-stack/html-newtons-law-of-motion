Newtons Laws of Motion

Newton’s Laws of Motion are three important rules that explain how objects move. These laws were created by Sir Isaac Newton in the 1600s.

First Law of Motion

The First Law says that an object will stay at rest, or continue moving in a straight line at the same speed, unless a force acts on it.

Second Law of Motion

The Second Law explains how force, mass, and acceleration are related.

The formula is: F = m × a

Third Law of Motion

The Third Law says that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Why Newton’s Laws Are Important

Newton’s Laws help us understand how the world works and how objects move.
